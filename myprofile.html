Background

I grew up looking up to a father and older siblings, who were engineers by profession. That was my early inspiration to be an electrical engineer. It was everything but unfortunate that I was forced to study education to teach physics in college; a poor country’s government coercing students in the name of ‘policy’. That was something that did not align with my childhood plan. I ended up teaching physics for high school students.


Early Experience with IT

I then joined one of the top CS schools in Ethiopia. The financial hurdle was not easy to overcome with the meager payment I used to receive as a teacher. I dropped out of the school. My journey then followed a different route into the nonprofit world and assumed many different roles for about 8 years. I am grateful for the opportunities and career paths that I took in the nonprofit organizations that I worked in.


The Journey

I moved to the US four years ago. I finished my graduate studies in Christian education. I started working for a nonprofit in Washington DC as a research fellow. But by of the end of 2018, I realized that I need to move to the tech world if I want a secure, good paying career. That was a huge adjustment in mindset and plans. I did not hesitate to join a coding bootcamp and took up to intermediate Web development in Html, CSS, and JavaScript. I was very fascinated about the capabilities of the new technologies and that opened my eyes that should be a serious consideration to stay viable and relevant in the job market in the coming decades. That decision motivated me to self-educated on different technologies and showed me the wide opportunities to work in tech and what I can do with what I learned. I even took the initiative to pay for very short-term trainings to further gain my programing skills. Further reflection & decision brought me to MIU. I hope to make the best out of my stay academic and career wise by being employed in educational technology world upon graduation.
